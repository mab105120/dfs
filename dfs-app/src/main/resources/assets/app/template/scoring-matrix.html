Scoring Instructions

On the next page you will be asked to use a 1-100 scale to evaluate teachers' performance on three job dimensions: Student Learning, Instructional Practice, and Professionalism. To better understand how to assign scores for each dimension please review the following details:

Student Learning

Teachers must provide consistent evidence showing that all students, including those with special needs, perform competitively on state standard exams for academic qualifications.

Evaluation Score Explanation
Exceeds expectation 75-100 All students show significant gains on standard exams.
Meets expectation 50-75 Most students show significant gains on standard exams.
Doesn't fully meet expectation 25-50 Some students show little gains on standard exams.
Below expectation 1-25 No evidence of gains on standard exams.
Instructional Practice

Teachers must provide consistent evidence indicating practice at the most effective level in the categories: • Planning and Preparation • Classroom Environment • Instruction

Evaluation Score Explanation
Exceeds expectation 75-100 Evidence of excellent classroom environment, preparedness, and clear instruction.
Meets expectation 50-75 Classroom is suitable for learning, lessons are planned, and instructions are clear.
Doesn't fully meet expectation 25-50 Evidence of some concerns with planning and instruction.
Below expectation 1-25 Unsuitable learning environment with no preparedness and poor instruction.
Professionalism

Teachers must provide consistent evidence of high level professionalism on activities including: • Professional Growth and Reflection • Collaboration and Engagement with the School Community • Communication with Families

Evaluation Score Explanation
Exceeds expectation 75-100 Proactive interaction with community, clear record of career development, and excellent relation with parents.
Meets expectation 50-75 Consistent collaboration with outside classroom activities, good career development potential, and close relation with parents.
Doesn't fully meet expectation 25-50 Infrequent interaction with community, slow career progression, and weak relation with parents.
Below expectation 1-25 No collaboration with community with very little growth and development.
